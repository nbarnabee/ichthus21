What is Ichthus 21?

  • A Christian (non-profit, non-denominational) organisation dedicated to the promotion of identity formation based on Christian values and beliefs.
  • Rooted in a Christian vision of God’s mission that is global in scope and ecumenical in perspective.
  • A response to the biblical mandate that in Christ God was reconciling the world to himself… and entrusting the message of reconciliation to us. —2 Corinthians 5:19

Our Mission

  • To foster the understanding and practice of peace, justice and solidarity within and between individuals, communities and Churches.
  • To inspire and support leaders, communities, and congregations to live as ambassadors of reconciliation in a world where differences (related to ethnicity, culture, gender, social status and/or religion) increasingly cause tensions between individuals, groups and nations.

What are Re/Conciliation studies?

Conciliation studies (from the Latin conciliare, to bring together) is a general term that refers to the process of bringing different parties into relationships of mutual benefit and enrichment, in order to live in a model of unity in diversity.

Reconciliation studies refers more specifically to the healing of broken relationships, the resolving of conflicts and wrongs of the past in order to reestablish and restore relationships.

Dr. Raymond Pfister, Founder

Originally from the Alsace region of France, Dr. Pfister has studied both in Europe and in the the United States. He holds an MA in Biblical Languages and an MDiv with a concentration in Christian Education. He received his PhD in Theology (University of Strasbourg, France) for his socio-historical research on the Pentecostal Movement in Alsace.

His wide experience across Europe includes the directorship of a Salvation Army centre, pastorates in the Pentecostal-Charismatic tradition in France and Germany, and positions at theological schools in Brussels (Belgium), Hamburg (Germany), and Birmingham (UK).
