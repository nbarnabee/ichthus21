What is Ichthus 21?

  • A Christian (non-profit, non-denominational) organisation dedicated to the promotion of European identity formation based on Christian values and beliefs.
  • Rooted in a Christian vision of God’s mission that is global in scope and ecumenical in perspective.
  • A response to the biblical mandate that in Christ God was reconciling the world to himself… and entrusting the message of reconciliation to us. —2 Corinthians 5:19

Our Mission

  • To foster the understanding and practice of peace, justice and solidarity within and between individuals, communities and Churches in Europe.
  • To inspire and support leaders, communities, and congregations in Europe to live as ambassadors of reconciliation in a world where differences (related to ethnicity, culture, gender, social status and/or religion) increasingly cause tensions between individuals, groups and nations.

What are Re/Conciliation studies?

Conciliation studies (from the Latin conciliare, to bring together) is a general term that refers to the process of bringing different parties into relationships of mutual benefit and enrichment, in order to live in a model of unity in diversity.

Reconciliation studies refers more specifically to the healing of broken relationships, the resolving of conflicts and wrongs of the past in order to reestablish and restore relationships.

Dr. Raymond Pfister, Founder

Dr. Pfister holds a doctorate in theology (PhD equivalent) from the University of Strasbourg, France. He has been involved in ministry since 1984, through several European churches and institutions, both in the UK and on the continent. This has for example taken place on campus with the Academy of Mission at the University of Hamburg (Germany) or online with King's Evangelical Divinity School (UK). His wide experience includes lecturing, dissertation supervision, pastoral counselling, and social work practice among chronically ill alcohol addicts. He has been married to Brigitte since 1992 and they have four children born while living in Hamburg, Germany.

After growing up as a French citizen in the province of Alsace, he has ministered in various cultural settings in a number of different European countries. He has ministered in English, French, German, and Italian. His local church commitment has led him to work within varied Pentecostal-charismatic settings. He is, since 2009, both an ordained presbyter with the Communion of Evangelical Episcopal Churches (USA), a Christian Convergent Communion, which blends the Evangelical (Scripture), Charismatic (Spirit), and Liturgical (Sacrament) streams of the Christian faith, and Canon theologian of the Order of Saint Leonard (UK), a global charismatic order inspired by the French Saint Leonard of Noblac and Celtic Christianity.
